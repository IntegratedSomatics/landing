About Me

"With each new step children take we bear witness to the profound unfolding and inseparable development of the mind and body, or soma."

Abi Bassler-Averitt

Bodyworker and Somatic Movement Educator

My passion for waking up has always lead me to teachers of contemplative practices and the healing arts. In college, yoga and Aikido practice seamlessly inserted itself into the center of my life and when an extended aikido injury brought me to a meditation cushion and the teachings of Chogyam Trungpa Rinpoche I simultaneously found myself on the doorstep of a healer and future mentor. With a copy of “Cutting Through Spiritual Materialism” tucked in my bag, I walked through her threshold and my tutelage in the healing arts began.

I became a Massage Therapist and immersed myself in the work Bonnie Bainbridge Cohen and Body Mind Centering. My desire to deepen my practice of meditation brought me to Boulder to be closer to Chogyam Trungpa Rinopche’s retreat center that later became my home. My connection to Tibetan Buddhist path and the teachings of Trungpa Rinpoche intertwined with my study of movement, dance and authentic movement. I pursued further education in the healing arts including Cranial Sacral Therapy, Somatic Experiencing Trauma Resolution, completed an extensive training in Body Mind Centering’s Somatic Movement Education, Infant Developmental Education and became a Feldenkrais Practitioner. The skillful means of complementary somatic therapies continues to inform, support and enrich my work to serve the diverse needs clients.
